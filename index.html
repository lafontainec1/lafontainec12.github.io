AbstractThis article discusses new technologies in the NBA that help players improve shooting. Systems now track each shot, analyze release angles, arc, and depth, giving instant feedback. It also highlights how young players benefit by receiving precise data instead of guessing, allowing faster development and more consistent performance during games.

Smart-Basketball & Shot Tracking Technology

Source 1

URLhttps://www.npr.org/2025/07/31/nx-s1-5485804/a-new-tech-company-makes-a-smart-basketball-that-can-help-you-become-a-better-shooter
AuthorNPR
AbstractThis article explains how a new smart basketball uses sensors to track shots and help players improve accuracy. The ball measures each throw and provides immediate data to the player via an app or coach interface. The technology focuses on improving form, shooting consistency, and overall performance. It is especially useful for players at all levels, from high school athletes to professional NBA players, making training more efficient and precise.

URLhttps://realsenseai.com/stereo-depth/tracking-nba-status-using-intel-realsense-technology/
AuthorRealSense AI
AbstractThis source covers how NBA teams use Intel RealSense technology to track player movements and shooting status during practice and games. The system provides real-time data on positioning, shot accuracy, and player movement. Coaches can evaluate performance quickly and adjust training plans accordingly. This technology gives teams a competitive edge by allowing precise measurement of skill improvement and physical execution.
Soccer Offside & VAR Technology

Soccer Offside & VAR Technology

Source 1

URLhttps://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/44038727/premier-league-fa-cup-semi-automated-var-offside-all-need-know
AuthorESPN
AbstractThis article explains semi-automated offside technology used in the Premier League and FA Cup. The system tracks player movements in real-time to determine offsides more accurately. It reduces human error and speeds up decision-making for referees. The technology combines multiple cameras and software to create a precise 3D map of players’ positions, ensuring fairer outcomes in critical moments like goals and scoring opportunities.

Source 2

URLhttps://inside.fifa.com/innovation/world-cup-2022/semi-automated-offside-technology
AuthorFIFA
AbstractThis source covers FIFA’s semi-automated offside technology used at the World Cup. It uses advanced sensors and cameras to track player positions instantly and assists referees in making correct offside calls. The system is designed to be fast, accurate, and unobtrusive, ensuring the natural flow of the game is maintained while improving fairness. It demonstrates how technology can support officials without changing the sport itself.
